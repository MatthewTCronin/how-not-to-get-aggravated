how not to

get aggravated

at everybody

(including yourself)

Look... the world is a frustrating place. People lie, deadlines are impossibly short, there's this little international health crisis going on, and you're supposed to deal with all of it on your own. This sit is NOT here to tell you your anger is invalid or unatural. But, you also do NOT need to shave your head and move to a monastary to find inner peace. Below are lists of simple ways to keep you from losing your temper with obligatory persons, with beloved persons, and most importantly, with yourself. Give it a try. Why NOT?

How NOT to get aggrivated with obligatory persons

(ex: obnoxious co-workers, an overly strict landlord, the jerk who ruined your morning coffee, etc.)

  1. Remove yourself from the toxic person or situation.
  2. Allow yourself to be angry.
  3. Pay attention to how that anger affects your mind and body.
  4. Understand you have control over your anger.
  5. Practice deep breathing or meditating.
  6. Try to pinpont why you are angry.
  7. Vent your frustration to someone you trust.

How NOT to get aggrivated with beloved persons

(ex: your uncle with obscene political views, your partner who just shrank your favorite shirt in the wash, your friend who got too drunk last night...again, etc.)

  1. Call time-out on yourself to reflect.
  2. Identify your the feelings your experiencing.
  3. Do a quick self-care check (i.e. Do you need food, water, and/or sleep?).
  4. Ground yourself and become aware of your present circumstances.
  5. Remind yourself and the person that this feeling/situation is temporary.
  6. Further ground yourself with frequent breathing excercises.
  7. Try to change your current environment and have a more playful attitude.
  8. Write a list of things to do that make you feel better.
  9. Relieve tension by physically relaxing your body, and sharing your feelings with the person(s).
  10. Talk about your feelings using language empathetic to you and your loved one.

How NOT to get aggrivated with yourself

(ex: you can't stay on task while you're working late, you decided to stay in when you know all of your friends are going out, you can't stick to the diet you started a month ago, etc.)

  1. Talk with someone you trust about these feelings (doesn't have to be a therapist).
  2. Talk out loud to yourself about these feelings.
  3. Write about your feelings (in a journal or otherwise).
  4. Recognize things you can't change, so then you can focus on the thing you can.
  5. Make big and little changes to reduce your anger and frustration.

Hopefully this site will help you in times when the world starts to get to you. And do NOT be afraid to read more with the links above.
